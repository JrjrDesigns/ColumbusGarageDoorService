Overhead door repair in Columbus

Overhead door and garage door refer to the same thing — a door that opens overhead, traveling up a set of tracks and resting horizontally under the ceiling. Whether you call it an overhead door or a garage door, we repair it.

We service all types of overhead doors in Columbus and Franklin County — residential sectional doors, commercial overhead sectionals, and roll-up steel curtain doors on warehouses and self-storage facilities. The repair categories are similar across all types: spring failures, cable issues, track problems, roller wear, and opener malfunctions.

Common overhead door repairs

Spring replacement

Torsion springs counterbalance the door weight. When a spring breaks, the door typically will not open. Spring replacement is our most common repair call and is typically completed same-day. Spring repair details

Cable repair

Lift cables run from the bottom corners of the door to the cable drums on the torsion shaft. When a cable snaps, one side drops. Do not run the opener on a door with a broken cable. Cable repair details

Off-track repair

Rollers come out of the track after an impact or when they wear out. A door off track should not be operated with the opener. Off-track repair details

Opener repair

Opener failures range from stripped drive gears to logic board failures to sensor problems. Most are repairable. Opener repair details

Emergency service

Door stuck open? Spring snapped? We take emergency calls nights and weekends at standard rates. Emergency repair

"When we get a call described as an overhead door problem it could mean almost anything. The diagnostic questions we ask are: does the door open at all, does it move unevenly or make noise, did you hear a bang, and is the opener motor running. Those four questions usually narrow the likely cause before we arrive and let us bring the right parts."
